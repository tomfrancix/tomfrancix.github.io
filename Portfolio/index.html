TILLASCOPE (ASP.NET)

I used ASP.NET to redesign a website i previously built with PHP. I decided to use a smaller color palet and focus on a user friendly UI. The cart stores data in session variables but also, if the user is logged in, their cart items will be stored for later. The security features of ASP.NET allowed me to enable an option for users to have two-factor verification via my Twillio account, by email or text. Users can also log in via Facebook and have the option to change their given email or password.

The database I created from the information I requested from the client has become quite extensive over time. There are hundreds of products each with their own properties, attributes, pictures, discounts, associations with other products and categories and groupings. Why not check it out?

VORTEX

Vortex is a social media platform that brings together people who love to post status's and photographs, write blogs and travel diaries, read news stories and engage in forum discussions. Vortex has it all layed out in discreet sections.

Every user must first register an account and log in before building their profile and sharing content.

Users can share different types of content in different areas of the website. The Stream displays all types of content in the order that it was shared. Several API's provide live feeds for statistics updates and general news reports. Sounds like a cool place if you ask me...
